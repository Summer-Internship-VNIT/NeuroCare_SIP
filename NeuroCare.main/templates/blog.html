üß† What Is Tuberculous Meningitis (TBM)?

TBM is a life-threatening infection of the protective membranes around the brain and spinal cord (meninges), caused by Mycobacterium tuberculosis. It is the most severe form of extrapulmonary TB.

üîç How Does TBM Develop?

TB bacteria can travel from the lungs to the brain through the bloodstream, causing inflammation and swelling of the meninges, which may lead to brain damage or death if untreated.

‚ö† Symptoms to Watch For

  • Persistent headache
  • Fever
  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Stiff neck
  • Confusion or altered mental state
  • Seizures
  • Coma (in severe cases)
  • In children: irritability, lethargy, poor feeding

üß† Why Early Detection Matters

Complications of untreated TBM include brain damage, hydrocephalus, stroke, and death. Timely diagnosis is crucial to prevent lasting harm.
